British Dominance in Formula 1 Racing


Formula One racing, the highest class of open wheeled racing has been around for 70 years. In that time thirty-three men from thirteen countries have been named drivers champion. Formula 1 also crowns the winningest contructor of the car's chassis at the end of the season. Constructors and drivers both come and go as their success waxes and wanes. Some have endured throughout the history of Formula 1 such as Ferrari and Alfa Romeo. Others were only around for a single season like the American Tec-Mec or the Italian Milano. Some like Mercedes come and go from the sport over the decades. One thing has remained constant though: the presence and dominance of British drivers and constructors in Formula 1 racing.

Constructors Championships by Team


First we will take a look at the constructors that have won a championship since Formula 1's inception. There have been only 15 teams to have won in the sports 70 year history. When first looking at this plot what stands out is that Ferrari has had ALOT of success. The italian outfit has been awarded a record sixteen titles. Almost twice as many as the next most successful team, Williams at nine. But Ferrari is the only Italian constructor to have ever one a championship. Ten of the fifteen victors have been British teams. France is the only other nation to have hosted more than one championship winning team.

Constructors Championships by Nation


Showing the proportion of the constructors who have won a championship by nation clearly shows the disparity between British teams and everyone else. 53% of championships have been won by a British outfit. In recent years Mercedes, a German manufacturer, has been hands-down the most dominant team in the field. They are currently on track to win their seventh consecutive title by the end of the 2020 season. But Mercedes has a long way to go before Germany closes the gap with teams based in Britain.

Driver's Championships


Now we look at the rockstars of the racing world. What makes the results of the drivers unique when compared to those of the constructors is that there are often non-British drivers racing in British cars and visa-versa. Even still the Brits reign as the nation with the most succesful drivers. Currently Lewis Hamilton, six-time world champion, is currently in the hunt for the retired German Michael Schumacher's record seven titles. The British driver is racking up more titles every year and is not showing any signs of stopping.

Driver's Championships by Nation


Again, looking at the proportion of championships sorted by the driver's country of origin reveals just how strong the British are in the sport. 27% of all drivers championships have been won by a British driver. Germany is at a distant second with 17%. And with Lewis Hamilton looking like he will continue to race for a few more seasons it's likely that the gap will only widen.
